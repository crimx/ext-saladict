      Dictionary
      mouse
      noun
      noun: mouse; plural noun: mice; plural noun: mouses
      /mous/
      1. 1.
        a small rodent that typically has a pointed snout, relatively large ears and eyes, and a long tail.
      2. 2.
        a small handheld device that is dragged across a flat surface to move the cursor on a computer screen, typically having buttons that are pressed to control functions.
      verb
      verb: mouse; 3rd person present: mouses; past tense: moused; past participle: moused; gerund or present participle: mousing
      /mouz/
      1. 1.
        (of a cat or owl) hunt for or catch mice.
      2. 2.
        use a mouse to move a cursor on a computer screen.
        "simply mouse over any item in the list"
